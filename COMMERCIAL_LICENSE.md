# Licensing Options

CollectorVision is published under the `AGPL-3.0`.

That is the default license for the public repository. If you are happy to
comply with the AGPL, you can use CollectorVision under those terms.

The copyright holder also offers separate licenses for users who do not want to
use the AGPL path.

## License paths

### 1. AGPL-3.0

Use this if you want the standard open-source path.

Typical fit:

- open-source apps and services
- research projects
- teams willing to comply with AGPL obligations

### 2. Free noncommercial license

A separate noncommercial license is available for hobby, personal, and similar
noncommercial projects that do not want AGPL obligations.

Typical fit:

- hobby tools
- personal projects
- unpaid experimentation
- noncommercial demos

If you publicly launch or distribute a project under that license, the project
requests, but does not require as a license condition, that you open an issue
or PR to announce it so it can be listed publicly.

### 3. Commercial license

A separate commercial license is available for proprietary and commercial use.

Typical fit:

- closed-source apps
- paid services and APIs
- internal business deployments that do not want AGPL obligations
- OEM and embedded deployments

## App/site deployments vs embedded deployments

CollectorVision uses two different commercial buckets:

- `Site/app license`: for normal software deployments such as web apps, APIs,
  desktop apps, and mobile apps
- `Embedded/OEM license`: for dedicated hardware such as scanners, sorters,
  kiosks, robots, or other shipped devices

If you are not shipping CollectorVision as part of custom hardware, you
normally want the site/app license rather than per-device pricing.

## Updates and renewals

Commercial licenses are intended to be perpetual for the versions covered by
the license grant.

Ongoing payments are for update eligibility, support, or expanded deployment
rights, not for continued permission to run versions you already licensed.

## Patreon and sponsorships

Patreon or sponsorship can be used as a payment channel and community perk, but
the actual software rights come from a separate license grant issued by the
copyright holder.

If you need a commercial or noncommercial license, be prepared to provide:

- your name or legal entity
- contact email
- intended use
- whether the use is hobby, commercial site/app, or embedded/OEM
- deployment quantity if hardware is involved

## Getting a license

Contact the project with a short description of your use case. The licensing
path depends on whether you want:

- AGPL
- free noncommercial use
- commercial site/app use
- embedded/OEM distribution

The public repository currently describes the intended licensing paths. Formal
alternative license texts may be published separately after review.
