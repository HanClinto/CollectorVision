# Commercial Licensing

CollectorVision is published under the AGPL-3.0. In short: if you use it in
a product or service you distribute or run for others, your code must also be
open-source under the same terms.

If that doesn't work for your situation, a commercial license is available.

## Who needs a commercial license

- Apps (mobile, desktop, web) that incorporate CollectorVision without
  open-sourcing the rest of the app
- Backend services or APIs that use CollectorVision to process requests
- Hardware products (card scanners, sorting robots, etc.) that ship with
  CollectorVision embedded

## Who doesn't need one

If your project is open-source under a compatible license, you're fine under
the AGPL. Personal projects, research, and internal tools that you don't
distribute are also fine.

## Getting a license

Send a note to [clinto@example.com](mailto:clinto@example.com) with a brief
description of what you're building. Pricing is based on use case and volume —
there's no one-size-fits-all fee schedule, and small independent developers
are treated differently than enterprise deployments.
